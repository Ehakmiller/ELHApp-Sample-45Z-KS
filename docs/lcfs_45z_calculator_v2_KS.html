Notes

The Section 45Z Clean Fuel Production Credit is a federal tax incentive that rewards producers of transportation fuels based on lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions. Fuels with lower carbon intensity receive larger per-gallon credits, creating a direct financial incentive to reduce emissions through process improvements, feedstock changes, carbon capture, and low-carbon agricultural practices. The credit applies to qualifying fuels produced in the United States for use as transportation fuel and is currently scheduled to run from 2025 through 2027, replacing prior biofuel tax credit programs.

The credit is earned on each gallon of eligible fuel produced and sold for transportation use. Producers can use the credit to offset federal tax liability or transfer it to other taxpayers under credit transfer provisions, converting it into cash value. Because the credit is calculated per gallon, total financial benefit depends on both the per-gallon rate and the facility’s annual production capacity.

Model Mechanics
  • Starts with a facility carbon intensity in gCO2e/MJ (LCFS CI or proxy estimate).
  • Applies adjustments (freight, ILUC, CCS, agricultural practices, misc) in gCO2e/MJ. Checked boxes are included in the calculations.
  • Converts to the 45Z policy unit kgCO2e/MMBtu at the final step for rate mapping.
  • Uses Benchmark, Slope, and Max Rate to estimate a $/gal credit rate.
  • Calculates annual value as rate × gallons using ethanol capacity.
  • LCFS delivered CI is treated as “delivered-to-CA” CI.
  • The tool estimates FOB CI by subtracting freight CI per mile × distance × multiplier.
  • 45Z proxy CI = FOB CI − ILUC (if checked) − CCS reduction (if checked) + other adjustment.
  • $ /gal policy mapping is parameterized and editable in V1.
